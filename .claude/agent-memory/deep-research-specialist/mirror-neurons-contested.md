**Verified:** Rizzolatti, Fadiga, Gallese & Fogassi (1996). Premotor cortex and the recognition of motor actions. *Cognitive Brain Research*, 3(2), 131-141. Found neurons in macaque area F5 that fire both during motor execution and observation of the same action.

**Caveats the story should acknowledge or not overclaim:**
1. Original finding was **motor**, not emotional — F5 is a premotor area.
2. Existence of a human mirror neuron system inferred from fMRI is **indirect**; direct single-unit evidence in humans is limited (Mukamel et al. 2010 is one of the few).
3. The "mirror neurons explain empathy / emotional contagion" extension is contested — see Hickok, G. (2014). *The Myth of Mirror Neurons.* Norton. Hickok argues the explanatory reach has been oversold.
4. Emotional contagion (Hatfield, Cacioppo, Rapson 1993) is better grounded in facial-feedback / autonomic synchrony evidence than in mirror neurons specifically.

**Why:** Story Act VII frames mirror systems as "the biological substrate of emotional contagion." This is the contested step. The Hatfield citation already in the story supports emotional contagion as a phenomenon; the mirror-neuron mechanism is the disputed bridge.

**How to apply:** If revising Act VII, consider hedging the mechanism: "mirror systems have been proposed as one substrate..." Also: the surgeon-anxiety claim ("predicts adverse events better than technical skill") in the same act needs a primary source — I could not verify it in May 2026 review. Likely a misremembered generalization of Sexton et al. 2000 on aviation/medical teamwork.
