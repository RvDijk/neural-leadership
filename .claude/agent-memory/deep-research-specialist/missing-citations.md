The story invokes several foundational ML/network results without citing the primary paper in its bibliography. Adding these would strengthen scientific credibility without changing the narrative.

**Should be added to bibliography:**
- **Srivastava, N., Hinton, G., Krizhevsky, A., Sutskever, I., & Salakhutdinov, R. (2014). Dropout: A simple way to prevent neural networks from overfitting. *JMLR*, 15(1), 1929-1958.** — Act XVII invokes dropout without citation.
- **Rumelhart, D. E., Hinton, G. E., & Williams, R. J. (1986). Learning representations by back-propagating errors. *Nature*, 323(6088), 533-536.** — Act XVIII invokes backprop without citation.
- **Vaswani, A. et al. (2017). Attention is all you need. *NeurIPS*.** — Act XVI invokes transformer attention without citation.

**How to apply:** When adding citations to the story, use these exact references; do not re-search them.
