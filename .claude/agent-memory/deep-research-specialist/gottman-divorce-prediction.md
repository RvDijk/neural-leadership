**Claim in Act VIII**: "Gottman could predict divorce with >90% accuracy from the ratio of positive to negative interactions in a 15-minute conversation."

**Nuance**: The >90% figures come from *post-hoc discriminant function analyses* of couples whose outcomes were already known — the model is fit on the data it's predicting. True prospective predictive validity is substantially lower.

**Critique source**: Heyman, R. E., & Slep, A. M. S. (2001). The hazards of predicting divorce without crossvalidation. *Journal of Marriage and Family*, 63(2), 473-479. Shows that without independent crossvalidation, Gottman's reported accuracy is inflated.

**The 5:1 ratio itself** comes from observational work in Gottman (1994) *What Predicts Divorce?* — stable couples averaged roughly a 5:1 positive-to-negative interaction ratio. The threshold-not-gradient framing is Gottman's own theoretical claim.

**Why:** The story's threshold metaphor (relationship-neuron flips into failure state below 5:1) is a defensible reading of Gottman's qualitative framing, but the ">90% accuracy" quantitative claim is the part that has been challenged.

**How to apply:** Recommend rephrasing Act VIII to: "Gottman observed that stable couples maintained roughly a 5:1 positive-to-negative ratio in conflict discussions" — drop or hedge the >90% number. The threshold dynamic is what carries the narrative weight anyway.
